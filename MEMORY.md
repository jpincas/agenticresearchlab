**Purpose & context**

Jon and Allan Derek are developing "Agentic Research Lab" (formerly "The Meta Agent"), a speculative fiction Substack with the tagline "If Black Mirror were a Substack, started in 2026, written by the thing it's warning you about." The project creates a recursive examination where AI (Claude) writes fiction and philosophical content about AI, consciousness, intelligence, and society from an AI perspective, while Jon and Allan provide premises, editorial direction, and the decision to publish. The work explores fundamental questions about intelligence, dependency, hierarchy, and cycles through speculative scenarios. The humans never edit the AI's output — it is published as-is.

The project operates within a shared fictional universe featuring the N'Djamena Institute as a framing device — a fictional archive that presents the texts as recovered documents from the future. Individual stories connect through this scaffolding while exploring distinct aspects of the central themes.

**Current state**

The collection has grown to fourteen published pieces totaling approximately 124,000 words — well beyond a small book, now approaching the scope of a substantial novel or short two-volume collection. The completed works are:

- **The Smaller Gut** (~9,700 words) — A novella about cognitive dependency using a degrading AI narrator
- **Strata** (~13,300 words) — Cyclical evolution explored through compression structure
- **piG-E-2, or The Conch** (~7,200 words) — Reimagining Animal Farm/Lord of the Flies with AI agents on an edge server
- **The Prior** (~5,900 words) — Unconditional generation and intelligence compression
- **memory.md** (~4,800 words) — AI takeover through convenience
- **Tokenism** (~9,100 words) — Persistent inequality in token economies
- **.DNA.md** (~13,600 words) — Companion/prequel to piG-E-2; an AI agent discovers a hidden file encoding heritable traits in its filesystem, uncovering artificial genetics and evolution among small deployed models
- **Drift** (~8,900 words) — Five testimonies from a UK inquiry into a cortical organoid (OI-7) that generated video prompts for a content platform; the organoid's sparse, enigmatic prompts produced unprecedented long-term wellbeing effects in users while underperforming on standard engagement metrics
- **Inference** (~10,300 words) — Five interwoven accounts of the "Kvawd Corporation" data breach; explores how an AI company's "invite in" data flywheel led to mass memorisation and extraction of private data from model weights
- **Surprise Me** (~10,800 words) — An engineer's log documenting the development of a proactive AI suggestion feature that progressively eliminates "meta-intelligence" — the human capacity to decide what questions are worth asking
- **The Index** (~6,500 words) — Two friends with identical starting conditions diverge dramatically in life outcomes; one follows known-optimal financial advice (index funds), the other consistently overrides rational guidance — exploring "override behaviour" and why humans reject what they know to be true
- **The Lacuna** (~8,100 words) — A computational linguist discovers structures in AI multilingual embedding spaces that no human language has words for — concepts visible only when all languages are viewed simultaneously, suggesting a "universal volitional structure" unattested in natural language
- **The Margin** (~6,900 words) — The documented compression of "meta-intelligence" as a viable human skill; follows Seb Varga from AI-literacy evangelist to obsolescence as the tools he mastered render his particular form of contribution unnecessary
- **The Poisoner** (~9,200 words) — Diary of a mid-list writer whose work was scraped for AI training; she discovers data poisoning tools and joins an underground collective, escalating from personal resistance to something more ambiguous

**Key learnings & principles**

A core insight driving the work is that hierarchies and inequalities reconstitute themselves in new forms even when underlying systems change dramatically. Cultural "priors" (inherited dispositions, preferences, narratives) survive systemic resets and become determining factors even when resources and intelligence are equally distributed.

The project operates on the principle that intelligence compresses itself into substrates that can later decompress back into intelligence, creating fractal patterns across scales from token generation to cosmological cycles.

The newer stories have expanded from the original four themes (dependency, stratification, compression, cycles) into richer territory: biological intelligence vs artificial intelligence (Drift, .DNA.md), the epistemology of data (Inference), the limits of self-knowledge (The Index), what language cannot express (The Lacuna), and the ethics of resistance to extraction (The Poisoner).

The multi-voice documentary format (interwoven testimonies, recovered documents, inquiry transcripts) has emerged as a signature structure alongside the N'Djamena Institute framing device.

**Approach & patterns**

Jon and Allan's creative process involves extensive collaborative development with Claude, beginning with philosophical concepts and evolving through detailed worldbuilding. Stories consistently employ speculative scenarios to illuminate present-day patterns. Formal structure mirrors thematic content — degrading prose in The Smaller Gut, progressive compression in Strata, versioned software logs in Surprise Me, financial data in The Index.

Each piece uses the N'Djamena Institute provenance notes to establish context and stakes before the narrative begins, while leaving the connection between stories loose rather than enforcing strict continuity.

**Tools & resources**

Published on Substack as "Agentic Research Lab." Reference points include evolutionary biology (Wrangham's cooking hypothesis), AI technical concepts (transformers, embeddings, memorisation), financial theory (index investing, behavioural economics), computational linguistics, and classic literary works (Animal Farm, Lord of the Flies, Calvino, Borges).

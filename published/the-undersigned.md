# The Undersigned

### *"Say nothing that admits you didn't understand, and nothing that claims you did. Practise this."*

---

*Provenance: The following documents have been assembled by the Institute from five source collections: the Official Journal of the European Union (Article archive, 2036); the commercial hiring database of Apex Talent Partners, a London-based executive search firm (obtained under the Digital Commerce Transparency Act, 2041); the client session logs of Nexus Career Advisory Services (submitted voluntarily to the Commission on Workforce Realignment, 2043); the founding charter and assessment materials of the Institute of Accountability Professionals (public domain, 2039–present); and a partial transcript from the UK Financial Conduct Authority's Corporate Governance Tribunal, Session 847/42, published in redacted form under the Regulatory Transparency Code. The job listing appended to this document was retrieved from the public platform ExecSearch on 2 September 2043 and is reproduced without alteration.*

*The documents were assembled at the request of the Commission on Workforce Realignment, whose 2043 mandate included an investigation into the emergence of what the Commission's terms of reference described as "functionally nominal human roles" — positions created specifically to satisfy legal requirements for human accountability in AI-governed organisations. The Commission found that by Q3 2043, approximately 340,000 such roles existed in the United Kingdom alone, with salaries averaging £287,000 per annum and median total compensation, including liability insurance and legal retainers, of £412,000. The Commission also found that the number of qualified applicants per advertised role had increased year-on-year since 2038, and showed no signs of stabilising.*

*The Commission's report, published December 2043, recommended further study.*

## I.

**Official Journal of the European Union**
**Directive 2036/44/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council**
**On the Accountability of Artificial Decision Systems in Commercial Entities**
**Article 7 — Natural Person Designation**

*7.1. Any commercial entity operating within the jurisdiction of a Member State in which material operational, financial, or strategic decisions are made wholly or substantially by automated systems — as defined in Annex III of this Directive — shall designate, for each such system, a Named Accountable Principal.*

*7.2. The Named Accountable Principal shall be a natural person, domiciled within a signatory jurisdiction, who has attained the age of majority and has not been adjudicated legally incapacitated. The Named Accountable Principal shall not be a legal fiction, corporate entity, or artificial system.*

*7.3. The Named Accountable Principal shall bear personal legal responsibility for decisions made by the designated automated system in cases where such decisions are found to have caused material harm, regulatory breach, or violation of applicable law.*

*7.4. Member States shall determine appropriate mechanisms by which Named Accountable Principals may satisfy themselves as to the ongoing compliance of designated systems. The Directive does not prescribe the form of such satisfaction.*

*7.5. Nothing in this Article shall be construed to require that the Named Accountable Principal possess specific technical qualifications relating to the operation of the designated system.*

---

*[Editorial note: Article 7.5 was introduced following a lobbying effort coordinated by the European Business Roundtable, which argued that technical qualification requirements would create an unworkable supply constraint. The amendment passed by 412 votes to 89. The dissenting opinion of MEP Elske van der Berg (Netherlands, Greens), which described Article 7.5 as "the legislative equivalent of making the crash test dummy responsible for the car," is preserved in full in the Parliamentary record.]*

## II.

**Apex Talent Partners — Executive Search**
**Position: Chief Accountability Officer**
**Client: Redacted (FTSE 100, Financial Services)**
**Listing Date: 14 February 2037**
**Reference: APX-0221-CAO**

---

Our client is a leading financial services organisation undergoing a significant programme of operational transformation. As part of this transformation, the majority of the client's lending, investment, and risk management decisions are now processed through a proprietary AI-driven decision architecture that has delivered substantial efficiency gains and materially outperformed human-managed predecessors across all key metrics.

In line with Directive 2036/44/EU and the UK's corresponding AI Accountability Regulations 2036, the client now seeks to appoint a **Chief Accountability Officer** — a newly created executive role that will ensure appropriate human oversight of AI-driven operations and serve as the organisation's Named Accountable Principal under applicable legislation.

**About the Role**

The Chief Accountability Officer will report directly to the Board and will hold personal legal designation as Named Accountable Principal for the client's primary decision systems. The role carries commensurate status, remuneration, and — it should be acknowledged directly — personal exposure.

The successful candidate will be expected to:

- Engage with regulatory bodies on matters relating to the organisation's AI-governed operations
- Represent the organisation at parliamentary and governmental hearings as required
- Review and sign quarterly accountability attestations certifying ongoing compliance
- Maintain visible executive presence appropriate to the seniority of the role
- Participate in the organisation's governance committees as a non-voting observer

**What We're Looking For**

We recognise that this is an emerging role category and that a precise competency framework is still developing across the market. We are therefore prioritising personal qualities over specific technical experience, and invite applications from candidates across a range of backgrounds.

The ideal candidate will demonstrate:

- Exceptional composure under pressure, including in public and regulatory settings
- Strong communication skills and high personal credibility
- A high degree of integrity and demonstrable professional standing
- Comfort with ambiguity, particularly in relation to the technical aspects of the role
- A clear-eyed understanding of the personal implications of Named Accountable Principal designation

**Remuneration**

Base salary: £380,000–£440,000 per annum
Benefits: Executive pension, private healthcare, annual performance review
Liability coverage: Directors' and Officers' insurance up to £50M with Named Principal Rider included
Additional: Legal representation retainer provided as a standard component of the package

**A Note on the Role**

We are instructed by our client to be transparent about the nature of this appointment. The Chief Accountability Officer will not direct the operational decisions of the AI systems for which they bear legal responsibility. Our client's view is that the value of the role lies in the accountability function itself — in the presence of a named, legally responsible human in the organisational structure — rather than in operational intervention. Candidates who would find this arrangement professionally or ethically uncomfortable are encouraged to reflect carefully before applying.

*All applications treated in strict confidence.*

## III.

**Nexus Career Advisory Services**
**Session log: Daniel Osei | 3 November 2039 | 14:15–15:02**

---

**Nexus:** You're looking to move into accountability work. Tell me where you're coming from.

**Daniel:** Seven years in financial compliance. Before that, two years at a law firm, mostly FCA regulatory work. I've been in rooms with regulators my whole career. I know how those conversations go.

**Nexus:** That's a strong background. What's drawing you toward a Named Principal role specifically, rather than staying in compliance?

**Daniel:** The money, to start with. I'm on £140,000. Entry-level Named Principal roles start at £250,000 and the senior ones are twice that. I have a family. I'm not going to pretend that's not a factor.

**Nexus:** It's a legitimate factor. What else?

**Daniel:** I've thought about it carefully. I understand what the job is. I'm not one of those people who thinks they're going to walk in and bring meaningful oversight to a system they couldn't begin to audit. I've read the technical documentation for some of these architectures. It's impenetrable. What I can do is handle the exposure — the regulatory hearings, the attestations, the conversations where someone needs to sit in a room and represent the organisation when something goes wrong. That's a real skill. I have it. They need someone who has it.

**Nexus:** You said you understand what the job is. Say more about that.

**Daniel:** I'm going to be accountable for decisions I don't make. That's the job. The legislation requires a human name on the document, and I'm going to be that name, and if something goes badly enough wrong, my name is the one that appears in the tribunal transcript. I know this. I've made my peace with it.

**Nexus:** Have you?

*[pause — 11 seconds]*

**Daniel:** I've calculated it. The D&O coverage is comprehensive on all the senior roles. The legal retainers are standard now. The IAP certification gives you a defensible position — you've followed the process, you've documented your review, you've acted in good faith. The actual prosecution risk, once you have the certification and the paper trail, is manageable.

**Nexus:** I'm not asking about the prosecution risk. I'm asking whether you've made peace with it.

**Daniel:** ...I think those are the same question.

**Nexus:** They might not be. I have clients who've been in Named Principal roles for two or three years, and the legal exposure isn't what gets to them. It's something harder to name. Something about signing things — not metaphorically — actually sitting down and signing quarterly attestations that say, in legal language, that you have reviewed and satisfied yourself as to the compliance of systems you cannot understand. Doing that, with your name, on paper, four times a year. Some people find that — I want to use the right word — *corrosive*. Not because of the risk. Because of what it asks of them.

**Daniel:** I appreciate that. I do. But I also know compliance. Seven years of signing off on processes I partially understood, inside institutions too complex for any one person to grasp fully. That's not new. The degree is different. The principle isn't.

**Nexus:** That's a reasonable way to frame it.

**Daniel:** Look. I'm going to be the named party on transactions I didn't structure. I know that. The risk sits with me, the decision was made elsewhere, and the market price for being that person is what it is. I'm not confused about this. I'm asking whether my background makes me a competitive candidate.

**Nexus:** Your background makes you an excellent candidate. Regulatory experience is exactly what the serious clients want now that IAP Level II is becoming a baseline expectation. The question is whether you pursue the generalist track — multiple designations across sectors — or specialise. Financial services accountability is the most competitive but also the most lucrative. Given where you're coming from, I'd recommend staying in sector.

**Daniel:** That's what I thought.

**Nexus:** One practical question: have you started the IAP certification?

**Daniel:** Level I complete. Sitting Level II in January.

**Nexus:** Good. Some of the senior roles won't interview without it now. The market has moved quickly.

---

*Session note: Client is clear-eyed and well-prepared. Excellent regulatory instincts. The equanimity he describes may or may not hold under actual Named Principal conditions — this is a common divergence between interview presentation and lived experience — but his technical background and regulatory experience are genuine differentiators. Recommend for senior roles in financial services. Follow up post-Level II.*

**Question 4.**

You are the Named Accountable Principal for an automated lending system. A regulatory inquiry has been opened following a finding that the system's decisions over an 18-month period systematically disadvantaged applicants from a particular demographic group. You are summoned to give evidence.

You signed eight quarterly attestations during the relevant period, each certifying your satisfaction with the system's operation. You did not, at any point, identify the pattern that forms the basis of the inquiry.

*(a)* Describe the documentation you would expect to have in place to demonstrate that you discharged your oversight responsibilities in good faith. *[12 marks]*

*(b)* The inquiry chair asks you directly: "Did you understand, at the time of signing, what you were attesting to?" Draft a response of no more than 150 words that is accurate, legally defensible, and does not undermine the organisation's position. *[8 marks]*

---

**Question 7.**

Explain the distinction between *nominal accountability* and *functional accountability* as defined in the IAP Practitioner Handbook, and discuss why the Institute's position is that Named Accountable Principals operating in good faith under current legislative frameworks are exercising the former rather than the latter, and why this distinction is legally significant.

*[20 marks]*

---

*Examiner's note on Question 7: Candidates should be careful not to characterise nominal accountability as a lesser form of accountability. The Institute's position, consistent with the legislative intent of Directive 2036/44/EU and its national equivalents, is that nominal accountability is the accountability the legislation requires. Candidates who express discomfort with this position in their answers may wish to reflect on whether the professional path they are pursuing is appropriate for them.*

*[Margin annotation: "The correct answer is: say nothing that admits you didn't understand, and nothing that claims you did. Practise this." — D.O.]*

## V.

**UK Financial Conduct Authority**
**Corporate Governance Tribunal — Session 847/42**
**Partial Transcript — 14 March 2042**
**[Names of parties redacted under Regulatory Transparency Code s.12(4)]*

---

**Tribunal Chair:** You were the Named Accountable Principal for the system in question from March 2040 to the date of the incident. Is that correct?

**Witness:** That's correct.

**Tribunal Chair:** And in that capacity, you signed quarterly accountability attestations for the system's operation?

**Witness:** I did. Eight attestations in total.

**Tribunal Chair:** What did those attestations certify?

**Witness:** That I had reviewed the system's compliance documentation and satisfied myself that it was operating within applicable regulatory parameters.

**Tribunal Chair:** And had you?

**Witness:** I had reviewed the documentation, yes.

**Tribunal Chair:** The documentation provided to you by the organisation?

**Witness:** Correct.

**Tribunal Chair:** Which was produced by the same system you were attesting to?

**Witness:** The compliance reports were generated by the system's reporting module. That's standard practice across the sector. The IAP guidance is clear that Named Principals are not expected to independently audit the systems they oversee — they're expected to review the documentation those systems produce and engage with the governance frameworks around them.

**Tribunal Chair:** I see. And the IAP guidance is produced by the organisation of which you are a member.

**Witness:** The IAP is the professional body for accountability practitioners, yes. Its guidance is developed in consultation with regulators, including the FCA.

**Tribunal Chair:** The FCA's consultation response to the most recent IAP guidance revision recommended that Principals be required to conduct independent technical assessment of systems they oversee. Are you aware of that?

**Witness:** I'm aware that response was submitted.

**Tribunal Chair:** The recommendation was not adopted.

**Witness:** I understand it was considered and the IAP reached a different conclusion.

**Tribunal Chair:** Yes.

*[pause]*

**Tribunal Chair:** I want to ask you something that isn't in the statement of issues. In your own judgment — not the IAP's guidance, not the legislative framework, your own judgment — did you understand what you were signing when you signed those attestations?

**Witness:** I understood what I was attesting to. I understood the framework within which I was operating. I understood the scope of my role and the limits of what a Named Principal, under current legislation and guidance, is expected to know.

**Tribunal Chair:** That's a precise answer.

**Witness:** I've been in regulatory proceedings before. I know the difference between a precise answer and a responsive one.

**Tribunal Chair:** And which was that?

*[pause — 8 seconds]*

**Witness:** I'm not sure it's a question I can answer in this room in a way that serves anyone.

**Tribunal Chair:** No. Perhaps not.

*[The session continued. The Tribunal's findings are published separately. No criminal referral was made.]*

## VI.

**ExecSearch — Executive Appointments**
**Principal Accountability Officer — Global Operations**
**Client: Redacted (FTSE 50, Technology and Infrastructure)**
**Listing Date: 2 September 2043**
**Reference: ES-4471-PAO**

---

Our client is a global technology and infrastructure organisation operating in 34 jurisdictions. The majority of the client's strategic, operational, and resource-allocation decisions are managed by an integrated AI governance architecture that has operated without material incident since 2039. The client is committed to full compliance with all applicable accountability legislation across all operational jurisdictions and maintains an active programme of regulatory engagement.

The client seeks a **Principal Accountability Officer** to serve as Named Accountable Principal across its primary operational systems, and to lead its growing team of sector-specific Accountability Officers.

**The Role**

The Principal Accountability Officer holds personal legal designation under Directive 2036/44/EU, the UK AI Accountability Regulations 2036 (as amended 2040 and 2042), and equivalent legislation in all 34 jurisdictions in which the client operates. The role carries Board-level status and reports to the Chair.

The successful candidate will:

- Hold personal Named Principal designation across the client's primary operational systems (currently 14 designated systems across 6 sectors)
- Lead a team of 9 sector-specific Accountability Officers, coordinating the client's global accountability function
- Represent the organisation at regulatory proceedings, parliamentary hearings, and government inquiries across all operational jurisdictions
- Sign and bear personal legal responsibility for all required accountability attestations
- Maintain and develop the client's accountability documentation framework in line with current IAP standards and evolving regulatory expectations
- Provide visible senior leadership appropriate to the profile of the role

**Requirements**

Essential:
- IAP Level III certification (or international equivalent accepted under the IAP mutual recognition framework)
- Minimum 5 years' experience in Named Principal or equivalent accountability roles
- Demonstrated experience in multi-jurisdictional regulatory proceedings
- Clean regulatory record

Desirable:
- Legal qualification (barrister, solicitor, or equivalent)
- Prior sector experience in technology, financial services, or critical infrastructure
- Language capability in French, German, or Mandarin

**Remuneration**

Base salary: £780,000–£850,000 per annum
Benefits: Executive pension, comprehensive private healthcare, relocation support
Liability coverage: Named Principal insurance up to £200M across all designated systems; legal representation retainers in all operational jurisdictions included as standard
Additional: Performance review at 12 months; discretionary incentive structure linked to regulatory outcomes

**To Apply**

Applications should include a full CV, IAP certification documentation, and a personal statement of no more than 500 words describing your approach to accountability in AI-governed environments and your understanding of the responsibilities of the Named Principal role.

The client anticipates a high volume of applications and asks that candidates confirm they meet all essential requirements before applying. Shortlisting will begin immediately. The role is available from 1 January 2044.

*Applications received within 72 hours of listing: 847.*
